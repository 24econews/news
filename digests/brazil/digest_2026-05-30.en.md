## Exame

### [CVM removes mandatory sustainability financial report requirement for publicly traded companies](https://exame.com/esg/cvm-retira-obrigatoriedade-de-relatorio-financeiro-de-sustentabilidade-para-companhias-abertas/)

The Securities and Exchange Commission of Brazil (CVM) has revoked the future requirement for publicly traded Brazilian companies to disclose sustainability financial reports. The change was formalized through CVM Resolution 244, issued on Friday the 29th, which amends CVM Resolution 193 — the rule that originally provided for mandatory adoption of international ESG reporting standards. With this decision, disclosure of such information becomes optional, easing the timeline for listed companies to comply with ISSB (International Sustainability Standards Board) standards. The measure represents a step back in the regulatory sustainability agenda for Brazil's capital markets, in line with trends toward reviewing ESG requirements observed in other jurisdictions.

### [Will U.S. decision on PCC and CV affect Pix? See questions and answers](https://redir.folha.com.br/redir/online/mercado/rss091/*https://www1.folha.uol.com.br/mercado/2026/05/decisao-dos-eua-sobre-pcc-e-cv-vai-afetar-o-pix-veja-perguntas-e-respostas.shtml)

The United States' decision to classify the PCC and Comando Vermelho as terrorist organizations has raised concerns within the Lula administration about possible impacts on Pix, the Central Bank's instant payments system. Brazilian authorities fear that classifying these factions in this category could pave the way for U.S. sanctions against the payment method, should financial transactions linked to these organizations be identified. Pix, which has established itself as the country's main transfer instrument, could face operational restrictions on transactions involving the U.S. financial system. The measure comes after Flávio Bolsonaro's visit to the United States and heightens diplomatic tensions between the two countries.
