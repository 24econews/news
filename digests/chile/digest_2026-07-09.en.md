The paradox reveals just how firmly expectations had anchored around a faster disinflation, and raises an uncomfortable question about the Central Bank's timing in a global environment that is once again turning complicated.

The number, strictly speaking, was not bad in absolute terms. Gasoline fell more than anticipated, which would normally have dragged the monthly CPI into negative territory. That it didn't reflects price pressures in other components of the basket that the market underestimated.
