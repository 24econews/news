Chile closed June with an economy now on its fifth consecutive month of contraction, a labor market at its weakest since the pandemic, and a political class urgently debating how to reverse a stagnation that increasingly threatens to harden into a technical recession.

The day's most striking data point came from the Instituto Nacional de Estadísticas: the national unemployment rate hit 9.4% in the March–May rolling quarter, its highest level since June 2021, with nearly 981,000 people out of work. The figure exceeded market expectations and deepened a landscape in which job creation—up just 0.8% year-on-year—cannot keep pace with the growth of the labor force. Female unemployment settled at 10.5%, and in the Región Metropolitana joblessness climbed to 9.8%, its peak since October 2023.
